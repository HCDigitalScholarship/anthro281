About Us

A class consisting of 23 students at Haverford College

This website was created in response to a lack of centralized information online surrounding the Gwich’in people and the fight against oil drilling projects. We are a group of learning students, not experts, putting back into the public a compilation of loosely annotated resources that we’ve found. We approach this project with a sense of humility around our own sense of positionality and acknowledge that we do not have all the answers. Our goal is to make the information that exists online more accessible to anyone and everyone who seeks it.

Our Team

Hey friends! We are the team responsible for this website. This was a final project for the Intro to Environmental Anthropology Class offered at Haverford College. We aim to spread and amplify the Gwich’in community and their voices.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Who are the Gwich'in?

    The Gwich'in are the northernmost Indian Nation living in fifteen small villages scattered across a vast area extending from northeast Alaska in the U.S. to the northern Yukon and Northwest Territories in Canada. Best known for their efforts of protecting the sacred place "where life begins"

  • What is the relationship between the Gwich'in and the caribou?

    The Gwich'in creation story tells that long ago, the Gwich'in and the caribou were one. As they separated into two beings, they became relatives and made an agreement. The land would sustain the caribou and the caribou would sustain the people.

  • What are Gwich'in activist aiming to protect?

    The Gwich'in Nation vows to protect sacred lands in the Arctic Refuge. The Gwich'in Nation vowed to protect sacred lands today after the Bureau of Land Management released a Final Environmental Impact Statement promoting oil & gas exploitation in the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge.

  • Why is it bad to drill for oil in Alaska?

    Offshore drilling for crude oil or natural gas in Arctic conditions has many inherent risks: immediate spills of the petroleum product, transport and release of invasive plant and animal species of drilling equipment, damage to wildlife, and discharge of trash and other debris.
